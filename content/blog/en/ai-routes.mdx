When you say "I want something cultural, calm, mid-morning," the app does not call Gemini right away. Six invisible steps run first.

## 1. Pre-filter

We take your location, current time and preferences. We filter the catalog to the places that match: open right now, in your category, within a reasonable distance.

## 2. Prompt builder

We build a structured prompt with the resulting candidates. It includes coordinates, hours, rating, estimated visit duration and constraints.

## 3. Gemini

We call Gemini with response forced to JSON. A schema validates the output: if Gemini drifts, we retry once with the same prompt, then degrade to a heuristic route.

## 4. Optimization

Gemini's output is reordered: approximate TSP by distance, adjusted to opening hours and declared energy level.

## 5. Enrichment

We add Storage photos, local tips and final metadata. Everything cached so the app does not make unnecessary trips.

## 6. Result

The `RouteResultActivity` screen shows four tabs: map, linear itinerary, drag-drop editor, and regenerate/share options.

> The user sees none of this. Just an itinerary that feels improvised by a local friend. That's the point.
