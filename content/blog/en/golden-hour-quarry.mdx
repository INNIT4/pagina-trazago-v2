The quarry stone of Álamos is not decorative. It was hand-cut from the nearby sierra during the 17th and 18th centuries and laid over adobe walls that were already a century old. It's a material that breathes: in the morning it looks beige, almost neutral. At noon it flattens. But between 5:30 and 6:10 p.m. — it turns gold.

## Why it happens

Pure physics. The sun, at that angle, crosses more atmosphere. Blues scatter and only reds and yellows reach the town. The quarry stone, already rich in iron and manganese oxides, gives them back amplified.

## Where to watch it

1. **The parish atrium.** Obvious — but with a trick: stand on the west side of the plaza and look at the main façade.
2. **Calle Comercio.** Walk south. The portales pull the light toward you.
3. **Cerro de la Cárcel.** If you arrive fifteen minutes early, the climb pays back in panorama.
